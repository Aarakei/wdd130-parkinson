The Pembroke Welsh Corgi is a remarkably sturdy and athletic herding dog, tracing its lineage back as far as 1107 AD when Flemish weavers brought the breed's ancestors to Wales. Despite their diminutive stature—typically standing only 10 to 12 inches at the shoulder—they were bred as "heelers," specialized in nipping at the ankles of cattle to move them across expansive pastures. This functional history explains their deep chests, surprising agility, and "low-rider" build, which allowed them to duck safely under a cow's kick. While they are famously associated with the British Monarchy, particularly Queen Elizabeth II who owned more than 30 during her reign, Pembrokes are distinct from their Cardigan Welsh Corgi cousins by their pointed ears and naturally short or docked tails. They are highly intelligent, often ranked among the smartest dog breeds, which makes them quick learners but also independent thinkers who require consistent mental stimulation. Their coat is a "double" variety, consisting of a weather-resistant undercoat and a coarser outer layer, which helped them endure the damp Welsh climate but also means they are prolific shedders.
